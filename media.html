Q. An adult male complains of abdominal distension. No history of past or current chest pain. On examination, temperature is 37.1°C, heart rate 78 beats/minute, respiratory rate 16 breaths/minute, and blood pressure 115/75 mm Hg. Fluid thrill is present. Imaging studies show a small cirrhotic liver and a globally enlarged heart. Which of the following cardiovascular conditions is he most likely to have?

A.  Severe occlusive coronary atherosclerosis
B.  Lymphocytic myocarditis
C.  Dilated cardiomyopathy
D.  Nonbacterial thrombotic endocarditis
